# OMNI-CORE — Execution Roadmap
## From Architecture to Operational Product

---

## Mission Statement

Build a sovereign epistemic operating system — governed knowledge infrastructure that survives contact with real operations.

Not demo theater. Not concept art. Not endless architecture.

**Execution phase begins now.**

---

## The 6-Step Execution Plan

### Step 1 — Freeze IAKE Core ✦

**Goal:** Lock Layer 1 so it stops drifting.

No more random upgrades to the core engine without formal justification.

Actions:
- Declare a freeze event for IAKE-1 schemas and governance rules
- Mark frozen artifacts in the registry
- Define the thaw protocol (what is required to reopen a frozen artifact)

Completion condition: `schemas/freeze_event.schema.json` in place, core schemas stable.

---

### Step 2 — Define Product Angle ✦

**Goal:** Choose the one institutional product claim that is most defensible.

Candidate: **Governed Knowledge Infrastructure** — claim tracking, dispute resolution, audit-ready logs.

Actions:
- Select primary target market (research institutions or AI governance firms recommended first)
- Define 3–5 core value propositions in plain enterprise language
- Write the v1 product brief (1 page, no fluff)

Completion condition: `docs/PRODUCT_BRIEF_v1.md` written and approved.

---

### Step 3 — Package v1 ✦

**Goal:** Create something a pilot user can actually run or evaluate.

Not a full product. A governed demo that is honest about its scope.

Actions:
- Define the minimal flow: submit claim → validate → audit log → view
- Wire Layer 1 (audit schema) + Layer 2 (claim schema) into one coherent demo
- Produce setup documentation
- Produce a one-page pitch that maps to the `ARCHITECTURE.md` pyramid

Completion condition: Demo runs end-to-end with real schema validation and audit emission.

---

### Step 4 — Pilot with 1–3 Real Users ✦

**Goal:** Get feedback from a person who is not the builder.

Pilot user profiles:
- A researcher who manages contested claims or sources
- A compliance officer who needs audit trails for knowledge changes
- An AI governance team that needs evidence-weight tracking

Actions:
- Share Package v1 with 1–3 pilot users
- Collect structured feedback using the `audit_event` schema as a model
- Identify what is confusing, missing, or broken

Completion condition: At least 1 external user has evaluated the system and returned structured feedback.

---

### Step 5 — Refine Governance Layer ✦

**Goal:** Close the gaps revealed by pilots.

Focus on:
- Schema clarity
- Approval gate usability
- Audit log completeness
- Dispute resolution workflow legibility

Completion condition: Governance layer updated based on pilot findings, new freeze event recorded.

---

### Step 6 — Expand Myth-Tech Layer Publicly ✦

**Goal:** Surface the cultural and symbolic depth of the system to a wider audience.

Only after Layer 1 is stable and Layer 3 has at least one paying or committed pilot user.

Forms:
- Educational content
- Symbolic tools
- Public documentation of indigenous knowledge frameworks used
- Workshops or course materials

Completion condition: At least one Layer 4 artifact published with clear attribution to the governed Layer 1 core.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Do not keep adding architecture documents before completing Step 3
- Do not delay pilots because the product feels "not ready enough"
- Do not let Layer 4 expand before Layer 1 is frozen
- Do not overengineer the demo
