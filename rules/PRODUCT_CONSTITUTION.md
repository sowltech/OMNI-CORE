# SOWL TECH — PRODUCT CONSTITUTION
## Monetisable Institutional Product — Layer 3 Rules

This document governs how OMNI-CORE functionality is packaged and presented as a commercial institutional product.

---

## 1. PURPOSE

Define what the Layer 3 product is, what it is not, and how it must be built, described, and sold.

This layer converts governed infrastructure into a product enterprises can evaluate, adopt, and pay for.

---

## 2. THE PRODUCT CLAIM

The product is:

> **Governed Knowledge Infrastructure**

It is not sold as a cultural tool.
It is not sold as a philosophy engine.
It is sold as governed epistemic infrastructure for high-trust organisations.

---

## 3. TARGET MARKETS

Primary targets:

- Research institutions managing competing knowledge claims
- AI governance firms tracking model decisions and evidence
- Policy labs handling contested evidence and audit requirements
- Corporate R&D teams requiring traceable decision provenance
- Legal compliance teams needing audit-ready knowledge logs
- High-trust organisations with formal dispute resolution needs

---

## 4. CORE PRODUCT CAPABILITIES

The product must demonstrate these capabilities:

| Capability | Description |
|---|---|
| Claim governance | Track assertions, their status, confidence, and provenance |
| Dispute resolution tracking | Formal workflow for recording and resolving knowledge conflicts |
| Evidence-weight arbitration | Structured comparison of competing evidence or sources |
| Audit-ready logs | Compliance-grade, immutable record of all knowledge mutations |
| Compliance change tracking | Full provenance chain for any governed record update |

---

## 5. PACKAGING RULES

### 5.1 Minimal viable package

A product is not packaged until it has:

- a one-page product brief (`docs/PRODUCT_BRIEF_v1.md`)
- a working end-to-end demo flow (submit → validate → log → view)
- a setup document
- a clear list of what is and is not included in v1

### 5.2 Honesty rule

Do not claim capabilities that are not implemented.

If a feature is roadmapped but not built, say so explicitly.

Pilot users must know what version they are evaluating.

---

## 6. COMMERCIAL DISCIPLINE RULES

### 6.1 No premature expansion

Do not expand Layer 4 publicly before Layer 3 has a committed pilot user.

Layer 4 expansion without a Layer 3 anchor creates a perception of vaporware.

### 6.2 Revenue focus

The product conversation must include:

- how the customer saves time, reduces risk, or improves compliance
- what the pricing model is (or what it will be)
- what a realistic pilot scope looks like

### 6.3 No overbuilding

The v1 product does not need to do everything.

It needs to do one thing reliably and auditably.

Identify that one thing. Build it. Pilot it. Then add.

---

## 7. PILOT PROTOCOL

A pilot engagement requires:

- a defined pilot user profile (researcher, compliance officer, AI governance team)
- a defined success criterion (what does "this worked" look like?)
- a feedback collection mechanism (structured, not ad hoc)
- a formal pilot close-out record

---

## 8. COMMUNICATION RULES

When communicating about this product to external audiences:

- use enterprise language: audit, governance, provenance, compliance, evidence-weight
- do not lead with cultural or mythic framing in commercial contexts
- the depth of the system can be referenced but is not the pitch
- the pitch is: governed, auditable, conflict-resolving knowledge infrastructure

---

## 9. LAYER DEPENDENCY

Product Layer (Layer 3) depends on:

- Layer 1 (Core Engine) for all schema validation, audit logging, and governance gating
- Layer 2 (Knowledge Mode) for claim, contestation, and provenance records

Layer 3 must not shortcut these dependencies to ship faster.

The governance infrastructure is the product. It is not separate from it.

---

## 10. SUCCESS CONDITION

The product layer is successful when:

- at least one non-builder user has completed a pilot evaluation
- the governance core produces real, reviewable audit logs for that user
- the user can explain what the product does in one sentence
- a second version can be defined based on real feedback, not speculation
